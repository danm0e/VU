The Technology

Enhanced Flight Vision Systems (EFVS)

An advanced equivalent vision set of technologies, allowing pilots to ‘see’ the departure, landing and surface environment during low visibility conditions.

EVS – Enhanced Vision System – is the sensor portion of the technology that includes cameras operating in different bandwidths of the frequency spectrum and a signal combining processor.

HUD – Head Up Display – is the viewing portion of the technology. It provides the pilot with an ‘out the window’ equivalent vision view that is normally seen during VFR flight conditions. Primary flight information from the aircraft avionics is overlaid on the enhanced vision scene.

Operation Approval – For EFVS to be meaningful to operators, the system must be approved, pilots trained and operator certified for low visibility runway activity. An essential part of EFVS is the operational approval.

How does Passive Millimeter Wave technology work?

Millimeter Wave technology can be passive or active. While active technology generates a millimeter wave signal that can then be reflected off terrain, passive technology senses sun generated, sky background signals, reradiated by the terrain. Different terrain radiates millimeter frequencies at varying intensity levels so cameras can sense and convert into a real time image.

Clouds, fog, dust, haze, rain, snow and other low visibility causing particulates are transparent to millimeter wave signals, this allows the PMMW camera to always see the terrain and runway visual cues required by pilots.

The sensor range and image quality is sufficient for pilots to operate into runways during all low visibility conditions. By combining an image from the infrared camera the overall result provides visual confidence from a more defined display of the airport environment.

Essentially, the PMMW technology is providing the ability for the pilot to predictably and reliably see essential visual cues, each and every time the aircraft operates at a runway. Other cameras that have less weather penetrating ability, fill in the surrounding detail, and the HUD provides the approved display mechanism.
