  • Reduce weather-related cancellations/delays, which:
    • Cost the U.S. airline industry over $7 billion per year in direct operating costs, averaging $78 per minute per aircraft.*
    • Cost the public over $35 billion in time lost, missed connections, and missed travel activity.**
  • Fundamentally change access and capacity to runways worldwide for equipped aircraft.

Equivalent Visual Operations is a disruptive innovation for aviation

  • For the airlines, it means reduced weather delay costs.
  • For the general public, it means a pilot can fly an aircraft looking out the window in all visibility conditions.
  • For the pilot, it maintains VFR like flight tempo during any weather—landing in zero visibility is a non-issue.
  • For the FAA, an effective Enhanced Flight Vision System (EFVS) could bolster the success of the FAA’s NextGen implementation plan to shift air traffic guidance from ground based to satellite.
